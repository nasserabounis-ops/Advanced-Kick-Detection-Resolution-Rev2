          Reference Library

          SCHEMATIC: FORMATION

          Porosity & Permeability

          Porosity: The void space between rock grains capable of holding formation fluid.
          Permeability (k): The ability of fluids to flow through interconnected pores (visualized by flow paths).
          SCHEMATIC: HYD HEAD

          Hydrostatic Pressure (HP)

          The pressure exerted purely by the weight of a stationary column of fluid. Notice how pressure vectors increase with depth (TVD).

          HP = 0.052 × MW × TVD
          SCHEMATIC: FORM PRESS

          Formation Pressure (FP)

          The pressure exerted by fluids contained in the pore spaces of rock. If Formation Pressure (Red) exceeds Wellbore Pressure (Blue), an influx (kick) occurs.

          Pressure Gradients

          The rate at which pressure increases with vertical depth (psi/ft). Normal saltwater gradient is ~0.465 psi/ft.

          Abnormal (Overpressure)

          Requires a sealing cap rock. Causes include under-compaction of shales, Artesian effect, and extensive gas caps.

          Subnormal

          Primarily caused by reservoir depletion. Introduces a high risk of severe lost circulation events.

          SCHEMATIC: U-TUBE

          BHP (U-Tube Concept)

          Static BHP:

          Pumps are off. Fluid levels balance perfectly. BHP equals Hydrostatic Pressure.

          Dynamic BHP:

          Pumps apply pressure (red arrow) down the pipe, overcoming friction to push fluid up the annulus. This dynamically increases bottom hole pressure (ECD).

          SCHEMATIC: ECD

          Circulating Friction

          1. Surface Lines (1-5%)

          2. Drill String (20-30%)

          3. Bit Nozzles (50-60%): Largest drop. Does not affect BHP.

          4. Annulus (APL) (5-10%): Most important. Acts downwards against rock, adding to HP to create ECD.

          Surface Pressure Limits

          MAASPMax Allowable Annular Surface Press

          The absolute maximum static pressure that can be applied to the annulus at surface before the weakest point fractures (based on LOT).

          MASPMax Anticipated Surface Press

          The highest pressure predicted to occur at the surface during a worst-case well control event. Used for equipment design.

          MAWPMax Allowable Working Press

          Manufacturer's maximum physical pressure rating for surface equipment.

          The "Big 6" Root Causes

          Operational failures or geological surprises causing pressure imbalances.

          Tripping Mechanics & Pumping Out

          The majority of well control incidents occur during tripping.

          Volumetric Monitoring
          • Monitor displacement every 5 stands.
          • Use dedicated stripping tank.
          • Prohibited: Continuous fill from active pit.
          String Configuration
          • Bit Balling multiplies swabbing suction.
          • Pumping Out: Break gel strength during POOH.

          Ballooning vs Kick

          Ballooning
          Kick

          Ballooning flow is FINITE and decelerates. Kick flow is CONTINUOUS.

          Flow Check Guide

          Eliminates dynamic variables (ECD) to reveal static balance.

          1. Prepare

          • Isolate Pit: Stop transfers/dumping.
          • Align Trip Tank: Use small tank for accuracy.
          • Drain Back: Wait for flowline to drain.

          2. Exec (Drilling)

          • Stop Rotary: Space out tool joint.
          • Stop Pumps: Removes ECD.
          • Observe: Monitor strictly for 10-15 mins.

          2. Exec (Tripping)

          • Set Slips: Secure string immediately.
          • Prep FOSV: Must be OPEN.
          • Observe: Check trip tank or annulus.

          RP-59 Diagnostics

          Differentiate Warning Signs vs Positive Indicators.

          Warning Signs

          Verification required.

          • - Drilling Break (ROP increase)
          • - Increased Torque & Drag
          • - Connection/Background Gas Spikes
          • - Decrease in pump pressure
          Action: Flow Check

          Positive Indicators

          Confirmed influx.

          • - Flow Rate Increase (Pumps constant)
          • - Pit Volume Gain
          • - Well flowing with pumps OFF
          Action: Shut-In

          Warning Signs

          May indicate swabbing.

          • - Excessive Overpull / Drag
          • - Trip tank level deviations (minor)
          • - Gas cut mud on bottom up
          Action: Flow Check

          Positive Indicators

          Confirmed influx.

          • - Hole fails to take calculated mud volume
          • - Hole returns more mud than calculated
          • - Well actively flowing
          Action: Stab FOSV & Shut-In

          API Shut-In Procedures

          Hard Shut-In methodology to minimize influx volume.

          While Drilling

          1. 1
            Sound Alarm.
          2. 2
            Stop rotating & space out joint.
          3. 3
            Stop Mud Pumps.
          4. 4
            Close Annular or Upper Pipe Rams.
          5. 5
            Open HCR Valve.
          6. 6
            Notify Supv & Monitor SIDPP/SICP.

          While Tripping

          1. 1
            Sound Alarm & Set Slips.
          2. 2
            Stab Full Opening Safety Valve (FOSV).
          3. 3
            Close the FOSV.
          4. 4
            Close Annular or Pipe Rams.
          5. 5
            Open HCR Valve.
          6. 6
            Open FOSV & Monitor.

          API Control Drills

          Muscle memory training for rapid barrier reinstatement.

          Pit Drill (Drilling)

          1. Simulate pit gain (alarm sounds).
          2. Stop rotation, pick up to space out.
          3. Stop mud pumps.
          4. Simulate closing BOP.
          Target API Time ≤ 1 Min

          Trip Drill (Tripping)

          1. Simulate incorrect fill volume.
          2. Halt operation, set slips immediately.
          3. Stab FOSV, then close it.
          4. Simulate closing BOP annular.
          Target API Time ≤ 2 Mins
